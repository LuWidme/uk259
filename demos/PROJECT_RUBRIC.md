# Day 5 Project - Grading Rubric

Total: **100 points**. Use alongside `demos/10_Project_Template.ipynb`.

| # | Area | Weight | Excellent | Solid | Needs work |
|---|------|:------:|-----------|-------|------------|
| 1 | **Data exploration & understanding** | 20% | Clear grasp of the dataset; meaningful plots; target and features correctly identified | Some exploration; basic plots | Little exploration; unclear target |
| 2 | **Cleaning & preparation** | 20% | Missing values handled; categories encoded; scaling where needed; sensible feature choice | Most steps done; minor gaps | Data not properly prepared |
| 3 | **Modelling** | 25% | Right model for the task; correct train/test split; model trained and runs | Model runs but minor issues (e.g. no split) | Model wrong for task or doesn't run |
| 4 | **Evaluation** | 20% | Correct metric; honest interpretation; mistakes & limitations discussed | Metric reported, little interpretation | No real evaluation |
| 5 | **Presentation** | 15% | Clear 5-min story; good visual; on time; handles a question | Covers the basics; rough delivery | Unclear or far over/under time |

**Grade bands:** 90-100 excellent - 75-89 strong - 60-74 solid - below 60: needs a follow-up chat.

**Guiding principle:** understanding beats accuracy. A 65%-accurate model the student explains honestly outscores a 95% model they can't explain.

## Presentation checklist (5 minutes)

- [ ] The question (what is being predicted, why interesting) - ~30s
- [ ] The data (dataset, size, one plot) - ~1 min
- [ ] The cleaning (what was fixed) - ~1 min
- [ ] The model & result (which model, how well, evaluation plot) - ~1 min
- [ ] What was learned (surprise, limitation, next step) - ~1 min
- [ ] Questions - ~30s
